Three directions for
Meeting Notes.

A monochrome rebrand for notes.jamesguldan.com, the relational system that holds every person, conversation, and project James touches. The brief: something incredibly modern, built on sacred geometry, in a strict palette of black, white, and grey, set on a white background. Below are three distinct ways to take the navigation and the page shell. Each is genuinely different in philosophy, not a recolor of the same idea.

Functional goal
Move fast through a dense tool used dozens of times a day. Navigation must be instant and never in the way.
Emotional goal
Feel like a precision instrument. Calm, exact, quietly expensive. Pride of ownership.
Who is looking
James daily as a power user, plus prospects on the Showcase page who need one genuine moment of awe.
The constraint
White ground, ink and grey only. Geometry must earn its place as structure, not stick on as decoration.

Design Direction

A clean white canvas where the Flower of Life supplies the proportional system. Column widths, nav spacing, and tile sizes all derive from the same set of intersections, so the layout has one logic rather than arbitrary numbers. The nav is a single hairline bar: wordmark left in a tracked grotesk, destinations right, each separated by a 1px hexagon node. The active item carries a filled vesica glyph and a one pixel underline. A Metatron lattice sits at ten percent opacity, anchored top right, bleeding off canvas. Hover draws a node from a single vertex in 150ms. Everything is hairline, every state change is one signal black.

Strategic Rationale

This is a tool opened dozens of times a day, so geometry has to be structure, not ornament. When every measurement traces to one system, the brand reads as rigor and intention rather than styling. The white ground keeps dense CRM data maximally legible and feels like fine stationery, and it modernizes the product without chasing a trend that dates in a year. It is the safest expression of the brief that still feels genuinely designed.

Design Direction

A bright white canvas. The primary navigation is radial: pressing the wordmark expands a centered mandala of concentric circles, a hexagram, and an inner hexagon, with the destinations arranged on its orbital ring. The active section sits in full ink; the rest in grey. The default page shell stays deliberately calm, a single centered column inside generous white margin, so the orbital menu is the one signature moment. The mandala turns almost imperceptibly, one revolution every several minutes. Grey does all the modeling, concentric strokes from eight to thirty two percent black.

Strategic Rationale

This is the most ambitious and the most memorable. It turns navigating into a small ritual, which is exactly the awe a prospect needs on the Showcase page. The trade is friction: a radial menu is slower than a fixed bar for a tool used all day, so it only works paired with keyboard shortcuts that let power use bypass the orbit entirely. Highest ceiling in the set, and the highest risk.

Design Direction

Pure white paper, charcoal ink. Sacred geometry appears only as delicate single weight line art: a Seed of Life logomark, hairline dividers that resolve into a vesica node, faint construction lines under headings. The nav is an airy top bar, the wordmark set in a high contrast display serif, destinations as wide tracked small caps over a single hairline rule. Pages breathe like an exhibition catalogue, large quiet headings and a measured reading column. Motion is almost absent, a single rule that draws itself in on load.

Strategic Rationale

The most timeless of the three and the most genuinely brand. It reframes a CRM as an editorial artifact, something James is proud to put on a screen in front of a client. Fraunces ties to his existing identity, so it reads as evolution rather than rupture. The risk is real: a dense data tool fights airy whitespace, so this direction only succeeds with disciplined information design that keeps scanning fast. The white palette is the most legible and the least likely to date.

Recommendation

Lead with Lattice. It is the only direction that treats sacred geometry as the actual structure of the product rather than a surface that sits on top, and that is what makes it feel modern instead of decorated. It serves the real job, a dense tool used all day, without asking James to trade speed for beauty. The strongest path forward is to ship Lattice as the working system, then borrow the Orbit mandala for the Showcase page alone, where one radial, ceremonial moment earns its awe in front of prospects. Atelier stays in reserve as the direction to revisit if the brand ever moves toward a lighter, more editorial public face.
