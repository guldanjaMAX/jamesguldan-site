Section 07 · Three Blessings

A sign off that sounds like him saying goodbye.

Three directions for the footer section. The job is to close the page the way Yanik closes a conversation. No Aho. Three blessings: one about the sun, one about the blooms, one about the sloth. The reader picks one. Or Yanik picks and it is already chosen. Grounded in how he closes calls on the Maverick community line and how he ends the 60 minute transcript.

Round
01 · three directions

The shift from v1. Stop trying to end the page. Let it breathe out.

The current v1 footer has the right structure but the voice is still a little formal. These three options work the same content through three different tones: ceremonial, intimate, and interactive. Copy is final. Layout is the question.

Option 01

The Three Suns

Three sun glyphs side by side. Each sun has a blessing under it in Fraunces italic. A single small sleeping sloth sits at the bottom of the composition. Reads as a final page of his journal with nothing more to say.

88
Confidence

Why this lands

Clarity. Symmetry. Three blessings get equal weight. The sleeping sloth is the period at the end of the page. The reader can read left to right and leave on the blessing that hit hardest. This is the most ceremonial of the three, which is also the risk.

May your suns find their suns. May your blooms outlive you. May the sloth in you rest easy.Three working blessings, derived from how Yanik closes

Design spec

Layout
3 columns, equal width, one blessing per column
Sun glyph
Hand drawn sun SVG from the library, 78px, burnt orange
Labels
Space Grotesk mono eyebrow: "for your suns / for your blooms / for your rest"
Body copy
Fraunces italic 22px, center aligned
Sloth
Small sleeping sloth doodle at bottom, centered, low opacity
Mobile
Stack to single column, sloth after third blessing

Option 02

The Handwritten Goodbye

Three blessings stacked as a short handwritten letter in Caveat. No glyphs, no grid. A signature at the bottom, a tiny sloth doodle as the period. The warmest version. Reads like the last page of a journal left on a table.

85
Confidence

Why this lands

Most intimate. Three blessings written as one closing note. No grid, no columns. The Caveat font gives every word the same hand. This is the version that reads as if Yanik just wrote it. Risk: a handwritten font over three lines can fight legibility on small screens. Needs careful line height.

I will write three things for you to take with you. You only have to keep the one that lands.Working voice for the section intro

Design spec

Layout
Single column, 640px, left aligned, 4px orange rule on left edge
Font
Caveat 28px, line height 1.55, ink color
Signature
Fraunces italic 22px, "yanik, still drawing"
Sloth
Large Caveat sloth doodle bottom right, 36px, low opacity
Mobile
24px Caveat, same spacing ratios
Accessibility
Alt text reads all three blessings as a single sentence

Option 03

The Three Doors

Three card shaped doors, one per blessing. Each door has an eyebrow label and the blessing. A handwritten "keep this one" line appears when the reader hovers. The page treats the blessing as a souvenir the reader chooses to take.

80
Confidence

Why this lands

Most interactive. The reader actively picks which blessing to carry. Clicking a door copies that line to the clipboard with a note "kept in your hand". The cards look like the page corners of a journal folded and pulled out.

Risk: interactivity at the end of a page can wake the reader up at the wrong moment. If the rest of the site is quiet, this section introduces kinetic energy that might feel off tone. Worth trying, most risky of the three.

You only have one pocket on the way out. Pick which one you are taking.Voice derived from the transcript closings

Design spec

Layout
3 cards, equal width, 20px gap, slight rotation per card
Corner fold
Triangular clip path top right, 40px, tan color
Hover
Card rotates to zero, lifts 3px, "keep this one" appears bottom right in Caveat
Click
Copies blessing to clipboard, micro toast reads "kept"
Fallback
Tap on mobile does the same, no hover state needed
Accessibility
Keyboard focus ring, aria label describes each blessing

Recommendation

Build Option 02 Handwritten Goodbye. Use 01 Three Suns as a print or share fallback.

Option 02 is the most Yanik. Nothing about the section is decorative. The reader gets three sentences in his hand and closes the tab. That is the goal. Ship this.

Option 01 Three Suns is a great treatment for an image render, share cards, or a printable keepsake. Keep it in the library.

Option 03 Three Doors is the highest risk, highest engagement version. Save it for a later pass once the rest of the site ships. It is a sharp idea that deserves a clean runway.
