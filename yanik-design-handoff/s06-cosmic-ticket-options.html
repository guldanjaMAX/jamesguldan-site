Section 06 · The Cosmic Ticket

Hand them a warm paper ticket, not a call to action.

Three directions for the invitation section. The job is to give the reader a seat, not to ask them for anything. Grounded in Yanik's Maverick1000 practice of invitation only gatherings, his belief that members "choose their own adventure", and Sophia's note on what a seat actually means once it is in your hand.

Myth Beat
The guide hands you a seat
Pattern
Warm paper, not a form
Voice Test
Does not beg. Hands something.
Round
01 · three directions

The shift from v1. A CTA that does not feel like one.

The current v1 Cosmic Ticket is the strongest concept in the deck and it renders well already. What these three options push is the micro moment: when does the reader realize the ticket is theirs. The answer shapes how the rest of the section breathes. All three keep the paper ticket metaphor and kill any language about signing up, joining, or applying.

Option 01

The Torn Ticket

A pristine paper ticket arrives already made out. Two fields on it: Name and Seat. The Seat field is pre filled with "one of a thousand suns". The Name field is blank with a handwritten "your name here" hint. A tear line separates the stub. Reader types, ticket signs itself.

90
Confidence

Why this lands

This option treats the ticket as already cut. The reader does not earn it, they find it already has their seat on it. The Name field is the only thing they have to write themselves. That single action of typing your own name onto a ticket that is already yours is the section's hero moment.

The tear line is real. The stub has a seat number that is actually meaningful: one of a thousand suns. The perf pattern between the main ticket and the stub is a small but important detail, not decoration.

A seat is not a thing you apply for. It is a thing someone saved for you before you walked in.Yanik voice, derived from the Maverick welcome overview

Design spec

Layout
Ticket 3:1 main to stub, dashed tear line, perforation pattern

Option 02

The Stamp Sequence

The ticket arrives blank. As the reader scrolls or hovers, stamps appear in sequence: YOU ARE A SUN, YOU ARE INVITED, YOUR SEAT IS SAVED. Final stamp animates a signature. Feels like the ticket is validating itself in front of the reader.

85
Confidence

Why this lands

Option 02 trades pristine for process. The reader watches the ticket get stamped. The drama is in the sequencing. Each stamp is a small moment of recognition. By the time the signature appears, the ticket has earned itself.

Risk: stamps can read as gimmicky if overused. The fix is to make each stamp a one time animation, not a loop, and to stop at three. No JavaScript carousel, no retry button, no resetting.

Some seats are saved quietly. Some you watch being saved in front of you.Voice derived from the transcript

Design spec

Layout
Same ticket base as Option 01, stamps layer on top
Stamps
Three stamps, appear 400ms apart on scroll into view
Stamp style
Burnt orange stencil border, rotated 4 to 8 degrees, slight smudge
Signature
SVG path draw of Yanik's signature, 600ms
Fallback
If reduced motion, all stamps appear at once
Mobile
Stamps trigger on initial visibility, no scroll parallax

Option 03

The Envelope

A cream envelope addressed in Yanik's handwriting to "the sun reading this". The ticket peeks out of the top. Clicking anywhere on the envelope opens it. The opening animation is slow on purpose. The ticket slides out, then the reader fills in their name.

81
Confidence

Why this lands

The warmest version. The envelope is addressed to the reader in Yanik's hand before they do anything. Opening it is an act. Then the ticket appears.

Risk: it adds a click before the ticket is visible, which is friction. On mobile the opening animation needs to be fast or it reads as gimmicky. Trade off is intimacy for friction. Option 01 already achieves intimacy without the extra step.

I still write letters. Every one of them gets a sun in the corner.Yanik · from the transcript

Design spec

Layout
Single envelope, 680px wide, centered on paper
Addressing
Caveat 26px, "to the sun reading this" + "care of here and now"

Reveal

Reduced motion
Skip animation, ticket appears in place

Recommendation

Build Option 01 Torn Ticket as the primary. Ship Option 02 stamp motion inside it.

Option 01 is the most honest. The reader sees a ticket that already has their seat on it. No stagecraft needed. Ship this as the main treatment.

Option 02 is not really a separate option. Use its stamp sequence as the micro animation inside Option 01. When the reader types their name, the ticket stamps itself "paid in presence" as a small reward.

Option 03 is a beautiful idea worth holding. A "letter from Yanik" microsite one layer deeper, maybe after someone accepts the ticket, could use the envelope pattern without clashing with this section.
