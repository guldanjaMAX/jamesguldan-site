**1. The core idea**

**The site is a myth, not a portfolio**

Yanik does not need a personal website that lists his books, his events,
and his LinkedIn. The internet already has those. What Yanik actually
carries, and what very few founders around him can see clearly, is a
coherent personal mythology that has been forming since he was a 14 year
old immigrant kid cold calling doctors for his father.

That mythology is the gift. The site should read like a hand illuminated
manuscript, written by someone who has been to the other side and come
back with a map.

> *"Waking up feeling full aliveness, vitality and magic. Being in the
> place of Creator, seeing your ideas create the ripples of impact they
> are meant to. Plant Seeds. Light Suns. Play with Infinite Blooms."*---
> Yanik Silver, Deep Work Interview, Phase 1

**2. Who Yanik actually is**

Before we can build a myth, we need to see the man underneath it. The
Deep Work Interview pulled out a Yanik most of his members and
collaborators never meet. Here is that Yanik, in his own words and in
the structural reads from his blueprint.

**Core wound**

Growing up as the son of Russian immigrants who spoke broken English,
Yanik had to translate and bridge worlds from childhood. The deeper
wound: feeling not quite from here but knowing he was meant for
something in service, and struggling for years to find words for that
calling before claiming his full identity.

Confidence: 0.85. Source: session\_insights.core\_wound.

**Origin story**

At 14, cold calling doctors for his father's medical equipment business.
At 16, a doctor client handed him a marketing cassette tape that turned
on the lights about direct response marketing. The real break came when
he realized he could move people with words, not as an escape from the
grind but as a survival tool that became a superpower.

**Transformation arc**

Immigrant kid translating for his father. Copywriting prodigy
discovering words move markets. Info publishing mogul with passive
income and status symbols. Spiritual seeker realizing the success felt
empty. Broke and broken, asking the journal: what am I really here to
do? Writing Light 1000 Suns with his non dominant hand. Building
Maverick as the expression of his mission. And now, at 52, integrating
every past iteration into one unified identity and one unified body of
work.

> *"Am I happy? Would I be happy doing this ten years from now? When I
> was honest in my journal, the answer was No."*--- Yanik, on the
> turning point
>
> *"I had a ticket to space and an Aston Martin. And when I had to
> choose, I kept the ticket to space."*--- Yanik

**Contrarian belief**

Synchronicities are love letters from the Universe across time and space
nudging you toward your greatest work. Most entrepreneurs ignore these
signals. Those who listen become the hands of the Universe and build
work aligned with their deepest purpose rather than just chasing scale.

**Feared self**

Staying on a path that looks successful from the outside (hot car, great
reputation, family) but feels hollow inside. Spending 10 more years
doing work that does not align with his soul. Becoming another scale
obsessed entrepreneur who never answered the call.

**Communication style**

Mixed and open. Initially polished and mission focused, but when pushed,
reveals deep emotional honesty and vulnerability. Comfortable with both
analytical frameworks and mystical language. Uses storytelling as his
primary teaching tool. Willing to go deep quickly once permission is
established.

Confidence across all of Section 2: 0.85. Every quote is verbatim from
the Deep Work Interview transcript. The structural reads are AI
extracted with 0.8 confidence markers.

At the bottom of the Maverick transition, Yanik was \$70K in debt,

the outside.

Confidence: 0.95. Direct quote in Phase 1 key quotes.

gesture: not everything on it needs to be perfect. Some of it should
look written by the other hand.

**5. Visual direction**

**Mood board, in words**

Alphonse Mucha meets John Steinbeck's Sea of Cortez meets a Wes Anderson
title card. Aged paper. Hand set type. Soft animations that feel like
pages turning, not like CSS transitions. The warmth of a well lit study.
The strangeness of finding a door behind the bookshelf.

**Color palette**

  --------------- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Parchment**   Background and primary canvas. A warm, off white paper tone. Never pure \#FFFFFF.
  --------------- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  --------- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Ink**   Primary text. Near black with a warm undertone. Feels like iron gall ink on aged paper.
  --------- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  --------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Gold Leaf**   Accent for initials, dividers, and sacred geometry overlays. Used sparingly, like real gold leaf.
  --------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ------------ --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Velvet**   A deep, saturated burgundy used for headings and ceremonial moments. The South American priest's robe.
  ------------ --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ---------- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Moss**   A deep, living green for the Seeds section. This is the plant kingdom color.
  ---------- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ----------------- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Jagua Black**   The deepest ink tone, reserved for tattoo style glyphs and signature marks.
  ----------------- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Typography**

  ------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Display**   A high contrast serif with real old style character, such as a revival of Caslon, Fell, or IM Fell DW Pica. The initials at the start of each chapter should be oversized and gold leafed.
  ------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ---------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Body**   A warm, legible book serif such as Spectral or Cormorant Garamond. Tight tracking. Generous line height. No compromise on readability.
  ---------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ---------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Hand**   One custom hand drawn face for margin notes, annotations, and the occasional quote pulled from the journal. This is where the non dominant hand lives.
  ---------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  --------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Forbidden**   No Helvetica. No Inter. No modern SaaS geometric sans. The second a default sans shows up on this site, the spell breaks.
  --------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Motion and interaction**

Motion should feel like a children's pop up book more than a software
interface. Scroll triggered elements emerge, unfold, bloom. The cosmic
sloth appears in the margins at unpredictable moments, sometimes
watching the cursor, sometimes yawning, sometimes holding a footnote.

There should be at least one hidden room (a trap door interaction, a key
shaped object that appears only on certain pages) that rewards the
curious visitor with a longer, more personal piece of writing. Alice in
Wonderland physics, applied to a personal site.

**7. Copy direction**

Every word on this site should pass two tests. First: would Yanik
actually say this out loud? Second: is it doing any work that an image
or a page of his journal could do better?

**Voice rules**

-   Use Yanik's actual phrases whenever possible: Plant Seeds, Light
    Suns, Play with Infinite Blooms, Radiant, Crowdsurfing,
    Synchronicities are love letters from the Universe, Cosmic alarm
    clock.

-   Favor the mystical word over the corporate one. Codes, not
    frameworks. Ceremony, not process. Garden, not portfolio.

-   Use the word Mission where others would say brand. Use the word
    Cycle where others would say year.

-   Short paragraphs. Often a single sentence. Silence between sentences
    is a design element, not a typography mistake.

-   Never use the word thought leader. Never use the word solopreneur.
    Never use the phrase high ticket on this site. These are tools of
    the trade, not part of the mythology.

**Opening line options, for you to react to**

> *"I was not born here. That turned out to be useful."*--- Opening
> option A
>
> *"At fourteen I learned that words could move strangers. At fifty two
> I am still figuring out where to point them."*--- Opening option B
>
> *"There is a version of my life where I own a faster car and no longer
> remember what I was for."*--- Opening option C

My pick (confidence **0.75**): Option B. It anchors the site in the
immigrant boy and the 52 year old mystic in a single breath, which is
the whole brand.

**Call to action language**

Never Apply, never Book a Call, never Subscribe. Instead: Write back.
Sit at the table. Light a candle. Join the garden. These are ceremonial
invitations, not conversion events. If we need a capture form, the form
lives inside a scroll, not a pop up.
