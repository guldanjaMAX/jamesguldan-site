## Part 2. Phone capture protocol

All of the above should be captured the same way. This is the exact protocol Yanik should follow. Confidence this produces production quality: 95%.

### Use a scanner app, not the camera app

The iPhone camera app applies HDR, denoise, and automatic sharpening that mangles marker lines. Scanner apps do the opposite: they flatten perspective, remove shadows, auto crop to the page, and output at 300 DPI or higher.

**Recommended apps (in order):**
1. **Microsoft Lens** (free, iOS and Android, best for this use case)
2. **Adobe Scan** (free, Adobe account required)
3. **Apple Notes built in scanner** (tap camera icon in a note, select "Scan Documents") — already on every iPhone, surprisingly good

### Capture setup

- **Surface:** Flat table, not a lap, not a bed, not a plane tray. Hardback of a book or a clean tabletop works.
- **Lighting:** Natural daylight near a window is best. Overhead room light second best. Avoid: direct sunlight (causes glare), lamp from one side (causes shadow on one side of page), fluorescent overhead (blue tint).
- **Phone height:** Directly overhead, 12 to 18 inches above the page. Keep phone parallel to the paper (don't tilt).
- **Page:** Flatten the journal fully. If a page curls, use a glass of water or a book to hold the opposite page down.

### In the scanner app

- Switch mode to **Document** or **Whiteboard** (never Photo)
- Let the app auto detect the page edges
- Confirm the capture when edges are green / locked
- If the app offers a filter: use **Original** or **Photo** (not "Greyscale" or "Black and White" — we want full color)
- Save as **JPG** at highest quality, OR **PDF** if the app only exports PDF (we can convert)

### File naming

Ask him to name files as he goes. Simple template:
- `symbol-sun.jpg`
- `symbol-seed.jpg`
- `cosmic-sloth-portrait.jpg`
- `flourishes-page.jpg`
- `signature-aho.jpg`
- `suns-variations-1.jpg`

If he can't rename on the phone, no stress — he can just shoot them in order and add a voice memo saying the order.
