**Part 1 • Positioning Thesis**

**The sentence the whole site serves**

> *Yanik Silver plants seeds, lights suns, and plays with infinite
> blooms. He redefines how business is played in the 21st century at the
> intersection of more profits, more fun, and more impact.*

Every decision on this site flows from that sentence. Home page
architecture, photography, typography, copy voice, even the way links
animate. If a module or phrase does not pull in the same direction, we
cut it.

Yanik already has two short-form positioning lines in the wild that
work. The first is his own: \"Your greatest work never feels like
work.\" The second is Tony Hsieh\'s endorsement of him: \"It is time for
evolved entrepreneurs, visionary creators, and change makers to rewrite
the rules of business for the 21st century.\" We keep both. The new site
reframes them inside a richer myth so they stop reading as standalone
slogans and start reading as chapter openings.

> **Why this matters** *The current yaniksilver.com positions him as an
> author with a book to download. The new site positions him as a figure
> people want to know, follow, and be pulled into orbit around. The book
> is still there. The book is just no longer the job the site is doing.*

Confidence in the positioning thesis: 0.90. The language, the symbols,
and the stories are all coming from his own mouth across multiple calls
and his Deep Work Interview output. The only open thread is whether he
wants the site to explicitly sell Maverick1000 applications or whether
it remains aspirational and gated. Default recommendation: aspirational.

**Part 2 • Where we are today**

**Audit of yaniksilver.com as of April 2026**

The current site was built on WordPress with Visual Composer around
2016, with copy that was last meaningfully edited in 2020. It is
essentially a one-page homepage plus a blog and a contact page, and it
is doing one job: collecting email addresses in exchange for the first
three chapters of Evolved Enterprise.

**What is working and must be preserved**

-   The tagline \"Your greatest work never feels like work.\" Iconic,
    confident, Yanik. Keep.

-   The positioning phrase: \"more profits, more fun, and more impact.\"
    Keep verbatim.

-   The bio line: \"Cosmic Catalyst, Maverick Mischief maker and
    Galactic Goofball.\" This is the voice in one line. Keep.

-   The lifetime goal: \"solving 100 of the world\'s most impactful
    issues by the year 2100.\" Mythic scale. Keep as a pillar.

-   The Maverick1000 roster name drop (Branson, Blakely, Tony Hawk,
    Blackwell, DeJoria, Hsieh, Russell Simmons, Tim Ferriss). Elevate
    from a paragraph to a proof module.

-   Hand lettered signature and doodle aesthetic. Preserve. Modernize
    the pairing, not the mark.

-   Tony Hsieh\'s endorsement. Preserve with gentle in memoriam
    treatment (\"the late Tony Hsieh, Zappos\").

**What is not working and must change**

-   Stack is fully legacy: Bootstrap 3, Visual Composer, universal
    analytics, Infusionsoft. We rebuild on modern stack.

-   Only one portrait of Yanik exists on the entire site. One. The site
    cannot feel like a personal myth without a body of imagery.

-   Only one call to action: download three book chapters. No path to
    Maverick1000, no speaking inquiry, no Cosmic Journal, no podcast, no
    booking, no newsletter archive.

-   Blog posts on the homepage are dated 2022 and 2023. We either reset
    the cadence or remove the feed.

-   The hero has no photograph. It is type on a cream doodle background.
    We need a hero that moves people.

-   There is no representation of the symbols that actually define
    Yanik\'s worldview: seeds, suns, blooms, the cosmic sloth, the
    tattoos, the Cebu awakening, the ticket to space question.

-   No video. No audio. No motion. A 2026 personal site for a man whose
    work is experiential should feel experiential.

Confidence in audit: 0.95. Based on full HTML capture of the live site
and cross referencing with his Deep Work Interview and six prior calls.

**Part 3 • Design Philosophy**

**The three principles the site is built around**

**Principle One • Mythic, not promotional**

The visitor should feel like they have opened a beautifully bound book
about a real person, not landed on a funnel. Sections read like
chapters. Photography reads like portraiture. Copy reads like a narrator
telling you the story of someone you are about to meet. The ask comes
later and it comes quietly.

**Principle Two • Editorial, not corporate**

2026 design consensus is clear. Editorial styling, human centered
imagery, generous typography, story driven layouts. We lean all the way
in. Think Kinfolk meets the Kennedy Library. Think a documentary film as
a website. Warm palette, serif display type, negative space as design.
This is the style Marie Forleo and Amy Porterfield have been moving
toward. It is what reads as premium in 2026.

**Principle Three • Playful, not precious**

Yanik will reject anything that feels stiff. His voice is Galactic
Goofball, his tool is a doodle, his idea of a business trip involves
running with bulls and putting on a mermaid tail. We build the elegance
first and then we let his doodles, his handwriting, and his marker
colors crash the party. The tension between the two is the brand.

> **Tone in one line** *Cosmic dignity with a mischief streak. If a
> museum curator and a carnival barker designed a site together, in love
> with the same story.*

**Part 6 • Visual System**

**The palette, the typography, the texture, the motion**

**Color palette**

The palette is warm, low saturation, and unmistakably earthbound. We
treat gold as a pigment, not as a metal. We treat black as ink, not as
digital black. Every color in the system has a reference to something
physical, which helps the design team stay grounded.

**Ink:** \#1A1814 --- warm near black, used for body copy and typography

**Velvet:** \#4A1F2B --- dark cranberry, used for display headings

**Gold:** \#C4703F --- burnt gold, used for accents, links, and CTAs

**Moss:** \#3E5641 --- deep botanical green, used for secondary accents
and the seeds symbol

**Parchment:** \#F5EFE4 --- primary background

**Mist:** \#E8E3D8 --- secondary background and cards

**Jagua:** \#0E0B08 --- the tattoo black, used sparingly for the tattoo
section

**Typography**

**Display:** Abril Fatface. Reserve for hero headlines and chapter
titles. 64 to 120 points. Preserves continuity with the current site\'s
iconic type treatment.

**Serif body:** Georgia or Lyon Text. Used for body copy, essays,
captions. 18 to 22 points.

**Sans labels:** Helvetica Neue, small caps, generous tracking. Used for
kickers, spec labels, and navigation. 11 to 14 points.

**Hand:** A custom web font of Yanik\'s actual handwriting, scanned and
vectorized. Used sparingly for his signature and occasional marginalia.

**Texture**

Paper is present but never decorative. Backgrounds use a subtle
parchment texture at 4 percent opacity, not visible on phones but felt
on a 27 inch monitor. Photography uses a slight warm grade with lifted
shadows. We avoid pure white backgrounds anywhere on the site.

**Motion**

Motion is whispered, not performed. No parallax. No cursor effects. No
scroll hijacking. Only three kinds of animation appear on the site: slow
fade ins as sections enter view, Ken Burns zooms on hero photographs,
and a gentle pulse on interactive elements. Total animation vocabulary
fits on a napkin.

**Grid and spacing**

Twelve column desktop grid with a max content width of 1280 pixels.
Generous vertical rhythm at 48 pixel base unit. Typography line height
set to 1.6 for essays. We design for reading, not for scrolling.

**Part 7 • Photography Direction**

**Yanik deserves a body of imagery, not a headshot**

The single largest gap in the current site is photography. We cannot
build a mythic personal brand with one author headshot from 2017. This
section is a full shot list and creative direction for a two day
editorial photography shoot that gives us a library we can draw from for
five years. All photography should be shot by a single photographer who
understands editorial portraiture. References: Annie Leibovitz, Platon,
Joe Pugliese. Not corporate headshot photographers.

**Day One • The man**

**The Threshold Portrait**

Full body. Cream linen shirt, sleeves rolled, his own jeans, no shoes.
Golden hour, outdoor, natural jungle or desert horizon. He is looking
off camera at something. This becomes the hero image on Chapter One.

**The Ink Series**

Macro photography of his tattoos against a warm neutral wall. Natural
light. We want to see the texture of his skin, the ink, the years. Five
to seven images. He wears a simple white tee or is shirtless,
photographer\'s discretion. This is art photography, not lifestyle.

**The Workspace**

Yanik at his desk. Not posed. Actually working. Marker in hand, doodling
on a pad. Warm window light. We want to see the mess. The drawer full of
markers. The sticky notes. The half drunk coffee. This becomes the hero
for the Journal and Doodles sections.

**The Laugh**

We specifically brief the photographer to get the laugh. Not a smile.
The full body, head back, shoulders shaking laugh. This is Yanik\'s most
distinctive feature on a call and it has never been captured properly.

**The Split**

The Mythic Bio section needs two contrasting portraits of Yanik shot
back to back with identical lighting: one formal, one candid. We shoot
them five minutes apart so the contrast is the same person, not the same
style.

**Day Two • The world**

**Community**

If possible we shoot at a Maverick event, or we recreate the feeling:
Yanik in a circle with three to five other people in conversation. Warm
indoor light. We do not shoot the event logo. We shoot the room.

**The Tools**

Still life of his notebooks, his markers, his Cosmic Journal, his book,
his passport, a plane ticket. Shot on a linen background, overhead,
golden hour indirect light. Three to five images. These become the
texture layer.

**The Stillness**

Yanik alone. In a chair. In a garden. On a porch. Eyes closed or looking
at the horizon. These are the Cebu and 100 Problems hero images. They
should feel like a still from a documentary, not a portrait.

**The Adventure**

One shot that captures the \"green speedo at 200 mph\" energy. Could be
him on a motorcycle, in a plane, in a pool in a gold suit, holding a
confetti cannon. We intentionally make one image that is a little
absurd. It earns its place because it is true to him.

> **Shoot output** *One two day shoot delivers roughly fifty hero
> quality images and eighty secondary assets. Budget accordingly:
> photographer day rate 3000 to 6000 dollars plus travel plus editing.
> Total typically 10000 to 18000 dollars for this caliber of output.*

**Part 8 • Copy Direction**

**How the words should sound**

The voice of the site is Yanik, sanded just enough that a reader who has
never met him still trusts it. Four rules:

**Rule one • Short sentences, warm rhythm**

No paragraph on the homepage is longer than sixty words. Most are twenty
to forty. We want the cadence of a friend telling you something
important, not a press release.

**Rule two • Specifics over adjectives**

Instead of \"visionary entrepreneur\" we write \"ran a hundred million
dollars through direct response before he was thirty\". Instead of
\"adventurer\" we write \"broke two hundred miles per hour in a race car
wearing nothing but a green speedo\". Specifics are the voice.
Adjectives are the absence of voice.

**Rule three • Mischief is a virtue**

Every long page gets one line that makes the reader smile. Not a joke. A
wink. \"He once surprised Sir Richard Branson in front of three thousand
people by walking on stage dressed as a showgirl. He has not been
invited back. He goes anyway.\" The mischief is what keeps the dignity
from becoming taxidermy.

**Rule four • Never sell yourself**

Yanik does not need to sell. The site should not try to. No \"as
featured in\" strips. No urgency timers. No \"join thousands of
entrepreneurs\". The tone is quieter than that and it earns attention
because it is.

**Sample headlines, in the house voice**

> *The myth of Yanik Silver, in seven chapters.*
>
> *More profits. More fun. More impact. He has spent thirty years
> proving the three are a chord, not a triangle.*
>
> *Plant seeds. Light suns. Play with infinite blooms. An operating
> system for a life that matters.*
>
> *One hundred problems. Solved by 2100. A man with a marker and a very
> long horizon.*
>
> *Letters from Yanik, once a month. Sometimes less. Never more.*

**Part 9 • Signature Moments**

**Seven ideas that make the site unforgettable**

A personal brand site that is technically correct is not worth building.
What makes a site great is a handful of moments that people screenshot
and send to friends. These are the candidates I would push for. We pick
the four that fit budget and ship the rest in phase two.

**One • The Myth of Yanik Silver, as a film**

On the hero, beneath the tagline, a small link: \"Watch the myth, two
minutes.\" It opens a two minute documentary style video about Yanik,
shot and edited to film grade. This is the one piece of video on the
site. It earns its place.

**Two • The Symbol Tree**

On the /seeds page, an interactive SVG tree. Each leaf is a seed Yanik
has planted that has become a bloom. Hover a leaf and it tells the
story: a person he mentored, a company he funded, a retreat that changed
someone\'s trajectory. This grows as he adds to it.

**Three • The Cosmic Sloth Cursor**

On the homepage only, and only on desktop, a tiny doodle of a sloth
appears as the cursor when the user reaches the Symbol System section.
It disappears when they scroll past. One signature moment of mischief.
Accessibility preserved because it is ornamental and appears alongside a
standard cursor.

**Four • The Handwriting Subscribe Confirmation**

Already drafted in the Invitation section. When someone subscribes, the
confirmation is a short sentence in Yanik\'s actual handwriting that
animates in as if being drawn. One sentence. That is the whole
confirmation.

**Five • The 100 Problems Dashboard**

A full page at /100 that is literally a living document of the one
hundred problems Yanik and his network have committed to solving by
2100. Each entry has a status, a partner, a dollar amount pledged. This
is his legacy document. It is also the best recruiting tool for
Maverick1000 ever designed.

**Six • The Roster as a Wall**

The proof section on the homepage doubles as a standalone page at
/roster. On that page, every name is clickable. Click a name and you see
the story of what Yanik and that person have built together. Some are
empty placeholders. The page literally tells you where to look.

**Seven • The Audio Marginalia**

Small icons beside certain paragraphs in the Journal let the reader tap
and hear Yanik read the next paragraph aloud. He does a twenty minute
recording session four times a year. It is the most personal touch any
site can offer and it requires no real time video or camera.

**Part 10 • Tech Stack**

**What we build it on**

**Recommended stack**

**Framework:** Astro or Next.js. Both support editorial content models
beautifully. Astro is simpler, cheaper, and faster. Next is more
flexible if we later add interactive tools.

**CMS:** Sanity.io or Contentful for the Journal, Stories, and 100
Problems. Yanik should be able to publish an essay in ten minutes
without touching code.

**Hosting:** Cloudflare Pages with workers for the interactive 100
Problems grid and form handling. James already runs on Cloudflare.

**Email:** ConvertKit or Beehiiv for the newsletter. Clean UX, creator
friendly, no Infusionsoft residue.

**Analytics:** Fathom or Plausible. Privacy respecting, simple
dashboard, no cookie banner required.

**Photography delivery:** Cloudinary or Imgix for responsive image
delivery and warm grade presets.

**Forms:** Cloudflare Worker that posts to GHL contacts so Yanik\'s
inbound flows directly into the CRM James already runs.

**What we deliberately do not use**

-   No WordPress. It got him here. It will not get him further.

-   No Webflow. Beautiful tool, wrong tool for an editorial site that
    will live for ten years.

-   No heavy animation library (Framer Motion is fine, GSAP
    unnecessary).

-   No chatbots. No pop ups. No exit intent modals.

-   No ad tech. No retargeting pixels on the homepage. Clean site.

**Part 12 • Positioning Alternatives**

**Three thesis directions, for Yanik to react to**

Before the first pixel is pushed, Yanik should choose one of these three
positioning directions. The entire site flows from this choice. I have a
favorite but all three are viable.

**Direction A • The Mythic Myself (recommended)**

Yanik as a figure. The site is about him, his worldview, his stories,
his symbols. Everything he has built lives inside that personal
narrative. This is what the plan above is based on. It is the most
ambitious and the most durable. It works for speaking, for Maverick1000,
and for legacy. It is the direction Seth Godin, Tim Ferriss, and Marie
Forleo have all chosen. Confidence 0.88.

**Direction B • The Invitation to the Club**

Yanik as the doorway to Maverick1000. The entire site is a premium
preview of the community. Every module drives to the application. This
is simpler, more commercial, easier to measure. It also risks reducing
him to a funnel. Confidence 0.65.

**Direction C • The Philosopher King**

Yanik as a thinker. The site leads with essays, ideas, frameworks.
Photography is secondary, writing is the product. Closer to Paul
Graham\'s site than to Tim Ferriss\'s. Works if Yanik plans to write
prolifically. Probably underuses his charisma. Confidence 0.55.

> **Recommendation** *Go with Direction A. It is the only direction that
> lets the Cebu story, the tattoos, the doodles, and the mermaid tail
> all belong in the same building.*
