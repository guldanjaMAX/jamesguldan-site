## Global guidance before you start

Five rules that apply to every section below. If a design choice breaks one of these, it is the wrong choice.

First, the cosmic sloth origin is the Bribri tribe of Costa Rica. Not Cebu. If a source file says Cebu, treat it as a bug and ignore. The myth polish brief has the corrected cosmology.

Second, every section must feel like a spread from Yanik's journal. Paper, ink, his hand, his colors. The deck is already established on cream paper #f6eeda with ink brown #2b2118. Fraunces for serif, Inter for sans, Caveat for handwritten captions. Do not drift.

Third, one hero per section. The A+ thesis is that each section carries exactly one world class idea. No second heroes, no backup panels, no "in case the first doesn't land."

Fourth, confidence scores are expected on every design decision. If you cannot score it, you are not done thinking about it.

Fifth, no dashes in visible copy. James's preference applies to every line you write.

## Section 01 The Thousand Suns (Manifesto)

**Myth beat:** The suns are scattered.
**Pattern:** The Luminous Vow.
**Design challenge:** Turn six sentences into something that feels like an oath.

**Source this from:**

`yanik-60min-transcript.vtt`. Yanik repeats a handful of core lines when he talks about the thousand suns. The repetition is the signal. Pull the ones that show up in three or more places.

`YANIK-IMPLEMENTATION-PLAN.md` section on "1000 Suns Manifesto" has the working draft.

`yanik-personal-brand-site-brief.md` has the earlier pass on positioning language.

**Assets to pull from the folder:**

The Self Activating Field of Suns (`IMG_1660` family). The Licensed to 1000 Suns sun icon library (`IMG_3252` family). The Journey Formula page with "Plant Seeds × Light Suns" (`IMG_2774`). One sun from the SVG library as the manifesto's anchor mark.

**How it should feel:**

Like handling a warm piece of paper. Not a corporate manifesto grid. One sentence at a time, paced, with room to breathe. Yanik's handwritten signature at the bottom. The page should go quiet around the words the way a room goes quiet when someone reads scripture.

**Confidence this section can land:** 94. The copy exists. The assets exist. The pattern is right.

**Open questions for the design pass:**

Does the reader see all six sentences at once, or does the page reveal them on scroll? Is there a single sun that brightens as the reader finishes the last line? Does the signature appear as an animated handwriting stroke, or already set?

## Section 06 Your Invitation (Cosmic Ticket)

**Myth beat:** The guide hands you a seat.
**Pattern:** The Cosmic Ticket.
**Design challenge:** A CTA that does not beg. It hands you something warm.

**Source this from:**

`Maverick_Welcome_Overview.txt` for how Maverick1000 describes membership.

`YANIK-PROJECT-HANDOFF.md` for the current CTA language.

**How it should feel:**

The guide is watching the field fill up with blooms, then turns and hands you a warm paper ticket. Your name goes in the Name field. There is a tear line. There is a stub. The ticket has been through his hands. It is not a form, it is a seat.

**Confidence:** 86. The metaphor is strong. The execution risk is that "paper ticket" designs often look like a theme park souvenir. Keep it journal.

**Open questions:**

What is the action when the reader clicks the ticket? A form modal would break the spell. Consider a direct mailto or a slow form reveal.

## Priority order for polish

If you can only work on three sections after the myth, do these in this order.

First, Section 01 The Thousand Suns. The manifesto is the emotional spine. If it does not land, the whole site tilts.

Second, Section 05 The Field of Blooms. The asset library is richest here and the pattern is clearest. This is the highest confidence win.

Third, Section 03 The Guide. First impression of Yanik the person. A weak one here makes the brightest suns harder to believe.

The Infinite Game and the Cosmic Ticket are the most interactive sections and will need more back and forth with Yanik before they are locked. Save those for after the structural three are landed.

## When you are stuck

Grep the transcript. `yanik-60min-transcript.vtt` contains an answer for almost every voice question. If a piece of copy does not sound like Yanik, it is because you have not checked whether he has already said it better.

Check the catalog. `MASTER_TESTIMONIALS_CATALOG.txt` has the words of 92 people who know him. If a section needs social proof, the proof is already written.

Read the plan. `YANIK-IMPLEMENTATION-PLAN.md` has confidence scores on decisions that were made before this polish pass. Treat those as defaults you can beat, not constraints.
