The Thousand Suns. Read the suns he left you.

Needs polish Confidence 90 Effort remaining one pass

The manifesto is not copy. It is the thousand suns. Each sentence is one of them, shaken loose from the Sloth's back and laid down on warm paper. The page goes quiet around it. His hand signs it at the bottom. The reader lowers their voice the way you do when someone hands you something warm.

A vow in six lines
I am not here to sell you a better version of what hurt.
I am here to remind you that the sun has always been free.
Your work is allowed to be joyful.
Money is a byproduct of how brightly you burn.
I am here to draw with you, to build with you.
If any of this quietly rings true, you are already one of us.

The Field of Blooms. Walk through his field.

Needs polish Confidence 92 Effort remaining one pass

Where each sun landed, a bloom rose. The blooms are the doodles. Not decoration, not assets. They are what grew when the suns touched the earth. We do not hang them in a polished grid. We pin them up on his studio wall, label them in his hand on torn tape, let them overlap, and leave a coffee ring next to one.

What is already there

  • Ticket copy drafted"Admit one / non transferable / paid in presence" and the invitation line are already strong.
  • CTA language"Take the seat" in place of "join now". Do not regress to the latter.
  • Sun icon libraryAvailable for the stub seal.

What is open

  • Click destinationWhere does Take the seat go? A form, a mailto, a scheduling link, a Calendly? Blocks build.
  • Name field behaviorTypographic only (looks like a ticket) or live input (user types name, ticket animates). Input is stronger, harder to build.
  • Stamp or no stampDoes the ticket arrive blank and get stamped when they click, or arrive pre stamped? Small call with big feel.
  • Serial number logicN° 1000 is pointing at Maverick1000. Consider if the number should evolve by reader.
Next design move
Before designing, answer one question with Yanik. Where does the ticket take the reader? Options: private list signup (mailto), Calendly to a Maverick intro call, or a waitlist form. The answer changes the section. Assume Calendly for now, design the ticket as static art with a primary button below, revisit input animation in a v2.
