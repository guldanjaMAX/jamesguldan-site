Section 05 · The Field of Blooms

Stand in his studio while the ink is still wet.

Three directions for the doodle gallery. The job is to make the reader feel they walked in while Yanik is still drawing, not that they opened a finished archive. 01 · three directions

The shift from v1. A wall that is alive, not an archive.

The current v1 Doodle Gallery is a clean grid, which is honest but flat. The best work in this section will feel like a guide is still drawing over the scans, like the pieces are held up with tape and might fall. Each option takes a different swing at that tension. None of them call this a gallery.

Option 01

The Studio Wall

Nine doodles pinned to an off white wall with washi tape, slight overlap, one coffee ring, handwritten masking tape captions. Feels like you walked into his studio while he stepped out to refill the kettle.

94
Confidence

Why this lands

Of the three, this is the one Yanik would draw himself. A studio wall is the pattern his own journal pages already use. Overlap is the tell. Pinterest grids do not overlap. Studio walls overlap because the newest idea covers a corner of the older one. This section should read like a guide who is more interested in making the next one than protecting the last one.

The coffee ring is a deliberate choice, not a joke. Yanik's journal pages have real coffee rings on them. This detail is his, not a stock texture.

I draw a sun every morning. A thousand so far. Whichever one is wettest is the one I care about.Yanik · from the transcript

Design spec

Layout
Absolute positioned doodles on cream wall, 9 to 12 pieces, overlap 15 to 30 percent
Tape
Cream washi strips, 56 × 20px, rotated per piece, torn edge
Captions
Caveat 14px on yellowed tape ribbons below each piece
Coffee ring
One, corner of the wall, semi transparent brown ring
Motion
Each piece nudges on hover, slight rotation, no lift
Mobile
Collapse to single column scroll with same tape and captions

Option 02

The Filmstrip

A horizontal filmstrip of doodles, one at a time comes into focus at the center, the rest soft blur at the edges. Scrolls on horizontal swipe. Feels like flipping through his journal one page at a time at the reader's own pace.

86
Confidence

Why this lands

Fast, focused, kind on mobile. The filmstrip format accepts one piece at a time so every doodle gets its own moment. The reader sets the pace. Good for users who want to digest slowly.

Trade: the filmstrip strips away the overlap and the coffee ring. It is more archive than studio. Reads clean but slightly less him.

Each one is its own spark. They are not competing, they are just taking turns.Voice extrapolated from the transcript

Design spec

Layout
Horizontal scroll, 18 frames, 180px wide
Focus
Center frame scales 1.08, burnt orange border, caption bolds
Edges
Frames outside focus softened with 40% opacity and 2px blur
Interaction
Native swipe, arrow keys, scroll snap on each frame
Captions
Caveat 14px, centered under each frame
Mobile
Swipe one at a time, larger touch target on focus frame

Option 03

The Garden Bed

A mosaic grid where each doodle sits in a plot. Hero plots are 2 × 2. The pieces cluster by theme: suns, blooms, sloth, game. Reads like looking down at a garden bed from above. Curated, composed, a little less alive than the studio wall.

89
Confidence

Why this lands

Most information density. Shows 12 to 18 doodles at once in a visually organized way. The 2 × 2 hero plots let a few pieces be primary. This option is the best for a reader who wants to scan first and commit second. It is the most website, the least journal.

Risk: gardens look best from above when weeded. Remove the messy, keep the composed, and you lose Yanik's edge. Studio Wall wins on voice, Garden Bed wins on efficiency.

Each one is a bloom. Some opened that morning, some still opening.Voice extrapolated from the transcript

Design spec

Grid
6 columns, plots are 1×1 or 2×2, 12 to 18 pieces total
Clusters
Suns top, blooms middle, sloth pieces bottom, game anchor right
Tags
Caveat caption in the bottom left of each plot on tape
Hover
Plot lifts 2px, shadow softens, caption lightens
Motion
Staggered reveal on scroll, 80ms delay per plot
Mobile
3 columns, 2×2 hero stays hero

Recommendation

Build Option 01 Studio Wall. Hold Garden Bed as a later catalog view.

Option 01 Studio Wall is the highest confidence win in the entire deck (94). It is the only one of the three that sounds like Yanik drew it. Overlap, tape, coffee ring, Caveat labels. Ship this.

Option 02 Filmstrip reads clean and mobile first. Keep in reserve if Studio Wall benchmarks noisy on small screens after build.

Option 03 Garden Bed is strong for a later archive page. If a visitor wants to see all 33 scans in one view, this is that page. Not the section, a deeper sub page.
