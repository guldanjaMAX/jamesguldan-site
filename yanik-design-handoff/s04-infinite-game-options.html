Section 04 · The Infinite Game

Show them the board, then show them their square.

Three directions for the Player Map section. The job is to make the reader recognize themselves as a player on Yanik's hand drawn board before anyone explains what the game is. Grounded in the Galactic Game journal pages, the mandala tattoo vocabulary, and Yanik's recurring line that the game is played by how you show up, not what you win.

01 · three directions

The shift from v1. Put the reader on the board first.

The current v1 player map is correct in structure but reads as a diagram, not an invitation. The fix is to make the reader find their own square before any explanation arrives. Each option below earns that moment differently. All three stay inside Yanik's actual tattoo vocabulary (seeds, suns, blooms, mandala, rays, sloth) and avoid any language about winning, scoring, or beating anyone.

Option 01

The Hand Drawn Board

A 7 × 3 grid sketched in ink on cream paper. Each tile holds one of Yanik's journal symbols. The reader's tile glows burnt orange with a handwritten YOU ARE HERE tag arcing above it. Two tattoo close ups pin the corners like field notes.

91
Confidence

Why this lands

This option resolves the core risk of the section: a board that reads as a product diagram instead of a guide's drawing. The grid is hand ruled, not CSS ruled. The tiles are not equal rectangles, they are wobbled squares in ink. One tile is alive, the rest are waiting. The reader lands on their own square before reading a single word of explanation.

The two pinned tattoo photos (sacred geometry mandala and sun face inner wrist) pull the board back into Yanik's body. This is the board he has already tattooed on himself. The reader is not learning a framework, they are being shown a map the guide has lived on for years.

I play one game. The game of lighting suns. Every tile on my arm is a reminder that the game keeps going.Yanik · from the transcript

Option 02

The Living Chart

A horizontal wall of overlapping journal scraps held together with washi tape. Each scrap is a tile, each tile is a symbol. The center scrap is larger, rotated toward the reader, and labeled in Yanik's hand with "you are here right now". The whole wall moves slowly on scroll like a mural.

87
Confidence

Why this lands

Option 02 solves a different risk: that the 7 × 3 grid will feel academic on mobile. A horizontal scrap wall compresses beautifully on narrow screens and gives the section a kinetic feel. The mural moves. The reader's tile drifts to center. It feels like a guide unrolling a map on a table.

Trade: the grid's geometric clarity is softer here. The reader gets motion and texture but loses the "I am a fixed point on a real board" feeling. This option earns its score on sensory impact, loses points on conceptual precision.

The board keeps scrolling whether you play or not. The only choice is which square you show up on today.Voice derived from the transcript

Design spec

Layout
Horizontal ribbon, ~24 scraps, 140px each, overlapping 18px
Focus
Center scrap scales to 1.12, rotates +2deg, burnt orange border
Washi tape
Cream tape 48 × 18px, rotated, across the top of every scrap
Scroll tie
Horizontal pan on vertical scroll, parallax factor 0.4
Mobile
Native horizontal swipe, snap points on each scrap
Caption
One Yanik line floats above the focus scrap in Caveat 20px

Option 03

The Constellation Map

Drop the grid entirely. Replace it with a dark paper starfield where each player is a sun at a different brightness. Thin gold lines connect the ones who have lit each other. The reader's sun is the largest, warmest, center of the field. Tap any sun to hear that player's one line.

83
Confidence

Why this lands

The riskiest option and the most memorable. It trades the Player Map's explicit grid for a field of suns where every named sun is a real Maverick, and connecting lines show who has lit whom. It is the most emotionally direct: the reader's sun is the warmest, which means "your light matters here before you earn it".

Risk: it drifts from the current 7 × 3 pattern into something closer to Section 02 Brightest Suns. There is a real danger of the two sections echoing each other. If picked, Section 02 would need a different visual treatment to hold the delta.

A sun lights other suns. That is the whole game. Nobody wins, everybody warms.Yanik · paraphrased from the transcript

Design spec

Background
Cream paper inverted to dark, radial gradient ink blue to deep brown
Suns
6 to 9 gold dots sized by brightness, 10 to 22px, named on hover
You sun
Center, 22px burnt orange, gold halo 3 layers, 4s breathing pulse
Connections
Thin gold lines at 20% opacity between lit pairs
Interaction
Hover any sun reveals its label and one short quote
Voice
Caveat labels, Fraunces italic title, no sans serif

Recommendation

Build Option 01 as the live section. Hold 03 for a later microsite.

Option 01 The Hand Drawn Board is the cleanest win. It keeps the 7 × 3 Player Map promise of the A+ deck, it puts the reader on a specific tile inside the first two seconds, and it pulls the tattoo photos back into the section so the board does not feel separate from Yanik's body. Ship this.

Option 02 Living Chart is the best mobile story. If the 7 × 3 grid feels too static after a build, swap in the scroll ribbon, keep the same symbols and the same highlight rule.

Option 03 Constellation is strong but overlaps with Section 02 Brightest Suns. Save it as a second gesture later, for example a members only map once a reader has accepted the Cosmic Ticket.
